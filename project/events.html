Featured Events

Sunrise Ridge Loop

Event date: Decembre 28 2024


Description: The Sunrise Ridge Loop is perfect for beginner riders and families. The trail follows a gentle, rolling path through lush forests, offering panoramic views of the Shenandoah Valley. The trail is mostly smooth with a few mild inclines, making it ideal for riders just getting started or those looking for a relaxing ride.


Key Features:

Difficulty: Beginner

Distance: 5 miles

Elevation Gain: 500 feet

Trail Type: Out-and-back

  • Views of the Shenandoah Valley
  • Perfect for first-time mountain bikers
  • Wildlife sightings (deer, foxes, and birds)

Creekside Flow

Event date: January 2 2025


Description:This intermediate trail offers a mix of flowing singletrack and more technical sections along the North River. Expect smooth climbs, fast descents, and a few rock gardens to keep things interesting. The creekside views are beautiful, especially during the spring melt, when the water levels are high. This trail is great for riders looking to challenge their skills while still having fun.


Key Features:

Difficulty: Intermediate

Distance: 10 miles

Elevation Gain: 1,200 feet

Trail Type: Loop

  • Flowing singletrack
  • Technical rock gardens
  • River crossings and scenic views

Ridge Run

Event date: January 13 2025


Description: For seasoned riders, Ridge Run offers a thrilling challenge. This advanced trail climbs to the ridgeline of Shenandoah National Park and offers jaw-dropping views along the way. Expect steep climbs, narrow switchbacks, and technical descents. The trail also features some challenging rock features and natural obstacles, making it a true test of skill and endurance.


Difficulty: Advanced

Distance: 12 miles

Elevation Gain: 2,000 feet

Trail Type: Loop

Key Features:
  • Steep climbs and descents
  • Technical rock gardens
  • Stunning views from the ridgeline

Stokesville Summit

Event date: January 22 2025


Description: The Stokesville Summit is an expert-level trail designed for those who seek a serious challenge. This trail takes you to the highest point in the region, with sustained climbs, rugged terrain, and high-speed descents. The trail is technical, with several sections of loose rock, sharp turns, and narrow paths that require focus and control. The payoff? Breathtaking views of the entire park and the valley below from the summit.


Difficulty: Expert

Distance: 15 miles

Elevation Gain: 2,500 feet

Trail Type: Out-and-back

Key Features:
  • Steep, rocky climbs
  • Technical downhill sections
  • Spectacular summit views
