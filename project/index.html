Mountain Bike Safety Tips

  • Wear a Helmet: It is the most basic but crucial piece of protective gear. Make sure it fits properly.

  • Protective Gear: Pads (knee, elbow, and gloves) can help prevent injuries, especially for more technical trails or downhill rides.

  • Know How to Fall: If you do fall, it is important to know how to protect yourself. Tuck your arms and try to roll rather than landing directly on your limbs.

  • Bike Check: Before each event, participants should do a quick inspection to ensure the brakes, tires, and chain are functioning properly.

  • Ride Within Your Limits: It is important to not push beyond your skill level, especially on unfamiliar trails or difficult terrain.
